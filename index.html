About

Myself

I work at the intersection of learning, UX, and product design, helping teams turn complex ideas into clear, engaging experiences. My background spans UX design, frontend development, product thinking, and language education—allowing me to move fluidly from research and strategy to execution and iteration.


I’m especially interested in how people learn, how design shapes understanding, and how thoughtful products can make technology feel more human. Outside of work, I’m usually traveling, learning new languages, collecting useless trivia, or doing standup comedy.

Focus

Where I add value

Learning & Experience Design

I help teams design learning-focused digital experiences that make complex ideas easier to understand and delightful to use. By combining UX, UI, and language education, I focus on clarity, motivation, and thoughtful interaction—so users don’t just move through a product, they actually learn from it.

Product & Technical Collaboration

With a background in frontend development, I work closely with engineers to shape features from idea to release. I help translate user needs into clear requirements, guide iteration through prototyping and testing, and ensure what ships balances usability, performance, and quality.

Content, Teaching & Storytelling

I create content that explains, engages, and sticks. Drawing from experience in teaching and public speaking, I blend structure, storytelling, and humor to deliver workshops, writing, or media that help people understand ideas—not just hear them.

Background

Crafting a learning-focused product perspective through lived experience

Much of my work has been shaped by learning—first as a learner, then as a teacher, and eventually as a designer and product collaborator. Studying and living in multiple countries, learning languages by immersion, and later transitioning into software development taught me how uncomfortable, motivating, and non-linear learning can be.


Those experiences influence how I approach product work today. I’m particularly interested in how people build understanding over time, where they lose confidence, and what helps them keep going. My background in UX and frontend development allows me to collaborate closely with engineers, while my experience in education and communication helps me advocate for clarity, motivation, and thoughtful iteration.


Rather than seeing design, technology, and learning as separate disciplines, I see them as parts of the same system. The most effective products don’t just function—they teach, guide, and adapt to the people using them. That perspective continues to shape the kind of work I seek out and the problems I’m most motivated to solve.

Journey

Experience that shaped my approach to learning and product

  • Programming & Design

    As a UX Designer and Front-End Developer I create and implement features which promote efficiency, simplicity, and exciting user experiences. I also earned a full-stack web development certificate from the University of Pennsylvania.

  • Clinical Research

    Leveraging my foreign language ability, I facilitated medical device trials by communicating with clinical teams in overseas research facilities. Navigating complex medical databases sparked my passion for programming.

  • Teaching

    Working in Beijing and Tokyo, I prepared business executives for international relocation. Comprehensive instruction covered material from negotiation through cultural etiquette. I conducted training in both English and German.

  • Learning

    Although my love of languages began much earlier, during 2008-2012 I completed a Bachelors Degree in languages (emphasis on German with a minor in Mandarin Chinese) and international business.
